1 · Platform Overview

Quant Desk is a full-stack quantitative research platform designed for systematic, factor-driven stock selection, portfolio construction, and risk analysis. It combines academic factor-investing research with practical technical analysis, all served through a fast browser-based interface.

Universes Covered
~12,000
US-listed tickers across NYSE, NASDAQ, and exchanges
Factor Models
13 Factors
Price-based + fundamental + macro
Analysis Modules
7 Screens
Overview, Screener, Factor Explorer, Technical, Backtest, Portfolio, Risk
Architecture at a glance Backend: FastAPI (Python) running on localhost:8000 — fetches market data from Yahoo Finance, caches in DuckDB, computes all analytics server-side.
Frontend: Next.js 14 App Router on localhost:3000 — React UI with interactive charts (Lightweight Charts, Recharts).

Core Design Philosophy

  • Factor-first: Every stock is scored on multiple dimensions (momentum, quality, value, etc.) that have documented predictive power in academic literature.
  • Cross-sectional z-scores: All factor scores are standardized to mean 0 / std 1 within the chosen universe, so you compare stocks relative to their peers, not on raw numbers.
  • Backtested evidence: The Factor Explorer lets you verify whether a factor has historically added value (IC > 0) before trusting it in live screening.
  • Integrated workflow: Screen → Explore factor quality → Backtest → Optimize → Risk-check — all in one platform without leaving the browser.

3.3 Factor Explorer

localhost:3000/factors
Validate whether a factor has historically predicted returns before trusting it in screening.

Two types of factors

Price-based (solid border) — Full IC history available Fundamental (dashed border) — Latest scores only

Price-based factors (Momentum, Volatility, Liquidity, Macro) can be back-computed historically from price data — so you can see how predictive they were in each past month. Fundamental factors depend on quarterly filings, which limits historical IC calculation.

Key Statistics

MetricFormulaGood threshold
Mean IC Average monthly Spearman rank correlation between factor scores and 21-day forward returns 0.02 – 0.08 (2–8%)
ICIR Mean IC ÷ Std(IC) — signal-to-noise ratio > 0.5 indicates a reliable signal
% Positive IC Fraction of months where IC > 0 > 55% (factor works more months than not)

Charts

  • IC Time Series — monthly IC bars + 3-month rolling average. Look for periods where IC collapsed — those are regime shifts where the factor stopped working.
  • Quintile Returns — stocks sorted into 5 buckets by factor score at each month-end, then tracked for 21 days. A monotonic Q1 > Q2 > Q3 > Q4 > Q5 spread confirms the factor has real predictive power.
Tip: Regime-conditional factors Momentum tends to have positive IC in trending markets and negative IC (reversal) after sharp sell-offs. Low Vol tends to outperform in high-VIX environments. Use the IC time series to understand when a factor works — not just whether it works on average.

3.5 Strategy Backtester

localhost:3000/backtest
Test whether a factor-based long-only strategy would have outperformed the S&P 500 historically.

Strategy mechanics

  • Universe: S&P 500 constituents
  • Selection: Top N stocks by chosen factor score at each rebalance date
  • Weighting: Equal weight within selected stocks
  • Rebalance: Monthly (end-of-month)
  • Benchmark: SPY total return
  • Transaction cost: User-specified round-trip bps applied on each rebalance turnover

3.6 Portfolio Optimizer

localhost:3000/portfolio
Given a set of tickers, find the scientifically optimal allocation using Modern Portfolio Theory.

How to use

  1. Paste comma-separated tickers from your screener shortlist (e.g. AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, JPM, XOM)
  2. Set a maximum per-position weight (default 25%) to enforce diversification
  3. Click Optimize — four methods are computed in parallel

Optimization Methods Compared

MethodObjectiveBest for
Equal Weight 1/N allocation Baseline comparison; surprisingly hard to beat out-of-sample
Max Sharpe Maximise (Expected Return) ÷ Volatility When you have high conviction in the return estimates
Min Volatility Minimise portfolio standard deviation Risk-averse portfolios; tends to concentrate in low-correlation assets
HRP (Risk Parity) Equalise risk contribution via hierarchical clustering Robust to estimation error; performs well out-of-sample

Output panels

  • Efficient Frontier Scatter — every possible portfolio plotted by risk vs return; the curve shows the maximum return achievable at each volatility level. Each method's portfolio is marked on the frontier.
  • Weight Table — side-by-side allocation for all methods. Large differences between Max Sharpe and Equal Weight signal the optimizer is making a strong tilt — verify the underlying assumptions.
  • Correlation Heatmap — dark red = highly correlated (redundant). If two holdings are >0.8 correlated, consider replacing one for a name that adds diversification.
Warning: Mean-variance sensitivity Max Sharpe allocations can be unstable — small changes in input data produce very different weights. The HRP method is more robust for live portfolios. Always sanity-check the Efficient Frontier and look for corner solutions (single-stock concentration) before using the weights.

4 · Fundamental Factor Formulas

All factors are computed cross-sectionally within the chosen universe and then z-scored (mean=0, std=1). Extreme values are winsorized at ±3 standard deviations to prevent outliers from dominating.

Z-score normalisation: Z(x) = (x − mean(x)) / std(x) Then clip: Z = max(−3, min(3, Z)) Composite Score: Composite = mean(all available Z-scores)

Momentum 12-1M

Captures the tendency of recent winners to keep outperforming. Skips the most recent month to avoid short-term reversal effects.

Momentum_12_1 = Price[today − 21 days] / Price[today − 252 days] − 1 Lookback: 252 trading days (~12 months) Skip: 21 trading days (~1 month)

Momentum 6-1M

Momentum_6_1 = Price[today − 21 days] / Price[today − 126 days] − 1 Lookback: 126 trading days (~6 months) Skip: 21 trading days (~1 month)

Low Volatility

Historically, low-volatility stocks have produced surprisingly competitive risk-adjusted returns — the "Low Vol anomaly".

DailyLogReturns = log(Price[t] / Price[t−1]) AnnualisedVol = std(DailyLogReturns, 252) × √252 LowVol_Score = −AnnualisedVol ← inverted: lower vol = higher score

Liquidity

DollarVolume[t] = Close[t] × Volume[t] AvgDolVol = mean(DollarVolume, 63 days) ← ~3 months Liquidity_Score = log(1 + AvgDolVol)

Macro Regime (Low Beta)

Beta = Cov(StockReturns, SPYReturns) / Var(SPYReturns) ← estimated over 252-day rolling window MacroRegime_Score = −Beta ← inverted: lower beta = higher score # Rationale: In risk-off regimes (high VIX), low-beta stocks are more defensive

Value

Value_Score = mean( −Z(Price / Book), ← inverted: lower P/B is cheaper −Z(Trailing P/E), ← inverted −Z(Price / Sales) ← inverted )

Quality

Quality_Score = mean( Z(Return on Equity), Z(Gross Margins), −Z(Debt / Equity) ← inverted: lower leverage = higher quality )

Profitability

Profitability_Score = mean( Z(Operating Margins), Z(Return on Assets) )

Earnings Revisions

EarningsRevisions_Score = mean( Z(Earnings Growth), Z(Revenue Growth) )

Sentiment

Sentiment_Score = mean( Z(6 − Analyst Recommendation Mean), ← analyst ratings: 1=Strong Buy, 5=Sell. Inverted so Buy = higher Z(Target Price / Current Price − 1) ← price-target upside )

Size

Size_Score = Z(log(Market Cap)) ← log to normalise skew

Information Coefficient (Factor Validation)

IC[month t] = Spearman_Rank_Correlation( FactorScores[end of month t], ForwardReturns[next 21 trading days] ) Mean IC = average(IC[all months]) ICIR = Mean IC / std(IC) ← signal-to-noise; target > 0.5 %Pos IC = count(IC > 0) / total months ← target > 55%

5 · Technical Indicator Formulas

RSI — Relative Strength Index (14-day)

Gains = mean(positive daily changes over 14 days) Losses = mean(|negative daily changes| over 14 days) RS = Gains / Losses RSI = 100 − 100 / (1 + RS) Interpretation: RSI > 70 → Overbought (highlighted red) RSI < 30 → Oversold (highlighted blue) RSI 40–60 → Neutral trend zone

MACD — Moving Average Convergence/Divergence (12/26/9)

MACD_Line = EMA(price, span=12) − EMA(price, span=26) Signal_Line = EMA(MACD_Line, span=9) MACD_Hist = MACD_Line − Signal_Line Interpretation: Histogram > 0 and rising → Bullish momentum building Histogram > 0 and falling → Momentum slowing (potential sell signal) Histogram < 0 → Bearish pressure

Bollinger Bands %B (20-day, 2σ)

Mid = SMA(price, 20) Std = rolling_std(price, 20) Upper = Mid + 2 × Std Lower = Mid − 2 × Std %B = (Price − Lower) / (Upper − Lower) Interpretation: %B > 1 → Price above upper band (extended) %B = 0.5 → Price at midpoint (neutral) %B < 0 → Price below lower band (extended/oversold)

Moving Average Distance

MA50_Distance = (Price / SMA(price, 50)) − 1 MA200_Distance = (Price / SMA(price, 200)) − 1 Positive → price above moving average (bullish) Negative → price below moving average (bearish)

ATR — Average True Range (14-day)

TrueRange[t] = max( High[t] − Low[t], |High[t] − Close[t−1]|, |Low[t] − Close[t−1]| ) ATR = SMA(TrueRange, 14) ATR% = ATR / Price ← normalised for comparing across price levels

Volume Surge

VolSurge = Volume[today] / SMA(Volume, 20) > 2× → unusual institutional activity (amber highlight)

Relative Strength vs SPY

RS_20d = (Price / Price[−20]) − (SPY / SPY[−20]) ← stock 20d ret minus SPY 20d ret RS_5d = (Price / Price[−5]) − (SPY / SPY[−5]) ← 5-day version Positive → outperforming the market over the period

Monthly Pivot Points

From prior month's High (H), Low (L), Close (C): PP = (H + L + C) / 3 ← Pivot Point R1 = 2×PP − L S1 = 2×PP − H R2 = PP + (H − L) S2 = PP − (H − L) R3 = H + 2×(PP − L) S3 = L − 2×(H − PP)

Weinstein Stage Analysis

Based on price position relative to 150-day (30-week) moving average: Stage 1 — Base: Below MA, MA flattening (sideways consolidation) Stage 2 — Uptrend: Above MA, MA rising, making higher highs and higher lows Stage 3 — Topping: Above MA but MA starting to flatten/roll over Stage 4 — Downtrend: Below MA and MA declining Best entries: Stage 1 → Stage 2 breakout Avoid longs: Stage 3, Stage 4

Coiled Spring Score (0–100)

Rewards (adds to score): + Tight Bollinger Band width (price compression) + Drying volume (sellers exhausted) + Price near 52-week high + Stage 2 classification + Positive RS vs SPY Penalises (subtracts): − Wide daily range (expanding volatility) − High volume without price progress − Far from 52-week high A high Coiled Spring score means: volatility compressed, momentum building, positioned for a potential explosive move.

Multi-Timeframe Alignment Score

Weekly signals (weight 40%): - Price above 10-week MA - 10-week MA slope positive - Weekly RSI(9) > 50 Daily signals (weight 35%): - Price above 50-day MA - Price above 200-day MA - RS vs SPY 20d > 0 Short-term signals (weight 25%): - Price above 5-day MA - 5-day return positive - Volume confirming Score = (weekly_hits/3)×0.40 + (daily_hits/3)×0.35 + (st_hits/3)×0.25 Final score scaled 0 → 100

6 · Portfolio & Risk Formulas

Portfolio Return & Variance

E[R_p] = Σ w_i × μ_i ← weighted average of expected returns σ²_p = Σᵢ Σⱼ w_i × w_j × Cov(Rᵢ, Rⱼ) ← full covariance matrix Sharpe = (E[R_p] − Rf) / σ_p

Backtesting Performance Metrics

CAGR = (Terminal_Value / Initial_Value) ^ (1 / Years) − 1 Sharpe = (Mean_Daily_Return − Rf) / Daily_Volatility × √252 (Rf = 0 in most contexts) Sortino = (Mean_Daily_Return − Rf) / Downside_Volatility × √252 where Downside_Vol = std(only negative returns) Calmar = CAGR / |Max_Drawdown| Max DD[t] = Portfolio[t] / max(Portfolio[0..t]) − 1 Max DD = min(Max DD over all t) Information Ratio = mean(Portfolio Return − SPY Return) / std(Portfolio Return − SPY Return) × √252

Fama-French 3-Factor Regression

Excess_Return[t] = α + β_Mkt × (Mkt-RF)[t] + β_SMB × SMB[t] + β_HML × HML[t] + ε[t] Where: Mkt-RF = Market excess return (SPY − risk-free rate) SMB = Small Minus Big: small-cap premium HML = High Minus Low: value premium (high Book/Market) Outputs: α = Annualised: intercept × 252 → unexplained alpha (skill) β_Mkt = Market beta (1.0 = same as market) β_SMB = Size tilt (positive = small-cap bias) β_HML = Value tilt (positive = value bias) R² = Fraction of return variance explained by the 3 factors t-stats = Statistical significance (|t| > 2 is roughly p < 0.05)

Value at Risk (VaR) & Conditional VaR

Historical VaR(α): Sort daily portfolio returns; take the (1−α) percentile e.g. 95% VaR = 5th percentile of the return distribution Parametric VaR(α): VaR = μ + σ × Φ⁻¹(1−α) where Φ⁻¹ is the inverse normal CDF Historical CVaR(α): CVaR = mean(returns ≤ VaR threshold) # Average loss on the worst (1−α)% of days Parametric CVaR(α): CVaR = μ − σ × φ(Φ⁻¹(1−α)) / (1−α) where φ is the standard normal PDF

Concentration Metrics

HHI (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index) = Σ w²ᵢ Range: 1/N (fully diversified equal-weight) to 1.0 (single position) Effective N = 1 / HHI Interpretation: equivalent number of equal-weight positions Example: HHI=0.2 → Effective N=5 (portfolio behaves like 5 equal positions)

7 · Recommended Research Workflow

This is the intended flow from market context to portfolio construction:

#StepToolOutput
1 Morning context — check macro environment, sector rotation, market breadth Market Overview Risk-on / risk-off decision; which sectors to favour
2 Universe screening — find stocks with the strongest multi-factor composite scores Universe Screener (S&P 500 or Theme) Top 20–50 candidate stocks by Composite Z-score
3 Factor validation — verify the factors you're relying on actually work in the current regime Factor Explorer Mean IC > 0.03 and ICIR > 0.5 gives confidence in the signal
4 Technical timing — from your screener shortlist, find stocks at actionable setups Technical Signals Screener (custom list) Stocks in Stage 2, above 50/200 MA, near pivot with volume confirmation
5 Portfolio construction — allocate optimally across your final list Portfolio Optimizer HRP or Equal Weight allocation (more robust than Max Sharpe out-of-sample)
6 Risk review — check factor loadings, concentration, and tail risk before going live Risk Dashboard Alpha > 0, R² < 0.9 (not just pure beta), CVaR within acceptable limits
7 Backtest validation — verify the strategy has historically worked Strategy Backtester Positive Sharpe, positive alpha, IR > 0.5 across multiple market cycles
Quick daily use (3 minutes) Market Overview → breadth check → Screener sorted by Composite → flag anything at a pivot on the Technical Screener. The full research workflow above is for new position initiation; daily monitoring is just steps 1 + 2 + 4.

8 · Glossary

TermDefinition
Alpha (α)Return in excess of what can be explained by factor exposures (e.g. market beta). Positive alpha = genuine outperformance.
ATRAverage True Range — measures volatility as the average daily price range, accounting for gaps.
BetaSensitivity of a stock's returns to the market (SPY). Beta = 1.5 means the stock moves 1.5% for every 1% market move.
Bollinger BandsDynamic price channel: 20-day SMA ± 2 standard deviations. Measures whether price is extended above/below recent average.
CAGRCompound Annual Growth Rate — the annual rate that would produce the same cumulative return.
Calmar RatioCAGR divided by maximum drawdown. Higher is better — measures return earned per unit of peak loss taken.
CVaR / Expected ShortfallAverage loss on the days that exceed the VaR threshold — the true tail risk metric.
DuckDBIn-process analytical SQL database used to cache all price and fundamental data locally for fast repeated access.
Efficient FrontierThe set of portfolios that offer the maximum return for a given level of risk (or minimum risk for a given return).
Fama-French 3-FactorAcademic model by Fama & French decomposing stock returns into market, size (SMB), and value (HML) factor exposures.
HHIHerfindahl-Hirschman Index — sum of squared weights. Measures portfolio concentration (0 = fully diversified, 1 = single position).
HMLHigh Minus Low — the value factor: return of high Book/Market stocks minus low Book/Market stocks.
HRPHierarchical Risk Parity — portfolio optimisation that clusters correlated assets and equalises risk contribution at each level of the tree.
IC (Information Coefficient)Spearman rank correlation between factor scores and subsequent returns. Measures how predictive a factor is.
ICIRIC Information Ratio = Mean IC / Std(IC). Measures the consistency (signal-to-noise) of a factor's predictive power.
MACDMoving Average Convergence/Divergence — momentum indicator using the spread between two exponential moving averages.
Max DrawdownThe largest peak-to-trough decline in portfolio value. The key risk metric for measuring how bad a strategy's worst period was.
RSIRelative Strength Index — oscillator (0–100) measuring the speed and magnitude of recent price changes. Used to identify overbought/oversold conditions.
Sharpe RatioExcess return per unit of total volatility. A Sharpe above 1.0 is considered good; above 2.0 is excellent.
SMBSmall Minus Big — the size factor: return of small-cap stocks minus large-cap stocks.
Sortino RatioLike Sharpe, but only penalises downside volatility (negative returns), not upside. Better for skewed distributions.
Stage AnalysisWeinstein's 4-stage model (Base / Uptrend / Top / Downtrend) based on price position relative to 30-week moving average.
VaRValue at Risk — the maximum expected loss over a period at a given confidence level (e.g. 95%). Does not capture losses beyond this threshold.
WinsorizingClipping extreme factor values at ±3 standard deviations to prevent outliers from distorting the cross-sectional rankings.
yfinanceOpen-source Python library that downloads market data from Yahoo Finance. Used as the primary data source.
Z-score(Value − Mean) / Standard Deviation. Standardises a number to how many standard deviations it is above or below the average of its group.
